What it does

Keep tiny asks on the page.

That open, paste, wait, and switch-back loop is fine once. It gets annoying when you do it again and again for short questions while reading, researching, or writing.

TabMate keeps the assistant on the page you already have open, so quick asks stop turning into a full browser detour.

Usual loop today

1Open a new AI tab
2Paste text and context
3Switch back to the page
20+ small ask moments in a normal day
3 steps removed from the usual loop

You do not need a new tab for every tiny AI question. TabMate keeps the answer flow attached to the page you are already on.

Why it feels better

The friction disappears in three places.

1

No extra tab hunt.

Ask from the page you are already on instead of opening a full AI tab and coming back.

2

No manual copy-paste.

The selected text goes out with the page title and link attached.

3

One setup across Chrome.

Pick one assistant once and keep the same default behind the corner button.

How it works

Set it once. Use it everywhere.

1

Choose ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

Pick the assistant you already use most. TabMate remembers it until you change it.

2

Click the corner button from any tab.

It opens a fresh popup chat without taking over the page you were using.

3

Highlight text for quick help.

Explain, Ask, and Simplify send the selected text with the page title and link already attached.
